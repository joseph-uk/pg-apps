Posted: Wed Jun 08, 2016 14:10 UTC    Post subject: Switzerland
According to “Guidance for HG and PG pilots visiting Switzerland“ on the Schweizerischer Hängegleiter Verband website, pilots who are not resident in Switzerland are allowed to fly here if they have an IPPI Card of level 4 or 5, and CHF 1,000,000 third party insurance. See https://www.shv-fsvl.ch/fileadmin/redakteure/Allgemein/Switzerland.pdf. The relevant section of the FAI website states that level 5 is required to fly cross country. See http://www.fai.org/ippi-card/recognition-and-use/373-civl/(shrinked URL)/31621-ippi-card-switzerland. When I fly in Switzerland, I carry (as well as my BHPA card and IPPI 5 licence) a copy of the current BHPA insurance details (which you receive with your membership card) as proof of the amount of cover.

And my understanding is that Andrew is correct - i.e. that if I were to become domiciled in Switzerland, I would then have to take an exam to get a Swiss licence; otherwise I would be breaking the law if I flew here.

Sorry, but you are to all intent and purpose incorrect: The law in England and Wales requires that you have the land owners permission to fly from their site. That is the limiting factor.

If you can get that permission without reference to the BHPA or any of their affiliated clubs then fine. However, most landowners require the level of insurance which only BHPA membership provides, and most flying sites are either owned or rented by BHPA affiliated clubs, so membership is effectively mandatory.

Most clubs will have a Nelson moment and turn a blind eye to visiting foreign pilots. Truth be told they should not.

To convert my European license and get the Australian equivalent one, all I had to do is to demonstrate that I can fly - so 1 flight with senior officer present was enough to do that. I had to show the officer that I can launch, fly, land, all safely, while following all rules. No written exam was needed.

As I am a visual, not a book learner, I have much more preferred to be tested this way. I'm not saying we should not be tested at all. Flying in Alps is far more dangerous than flying in the UK and one should have a solid Alpine knowledge before attempting to do so.
