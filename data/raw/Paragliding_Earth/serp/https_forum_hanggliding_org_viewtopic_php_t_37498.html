MAPS (All Feature Downloadable KML Files):

Hang Gliding Flight Schools & Clubs:
Clearly organized, detailed and complete map of all hang gliding schools and clubs around the world. In the United States alone, nearly 50 schools and 60 clubs are featured, including links to all their websites.
https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mi ... 244592&z=5
Also available for download at http://www.hangglidingflightschool.com/about.php

Global List of Flying Sites:
This map is massive, but very disorganized. Some of the map markers display a ton of information while others are literally nothing more than a name. Focused on flying sites, but does not distinguish schools or clubs.
https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mi ... 845719&z=6

Bay Area Fledglings Map:
Strange map combing flying sites, schools and clubs in a very haphazard manner.
https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mi ... 181538&z=7

USHPA Chapter Sites:
Very incomplete map which is missing numerous hang gliding schools/clubs and contains a lot of paragliding sites.
https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mi ... 742865&z=4

PASA Schools:
Very sparse map with very little information. Only shows about 11 hang gliding schools and a few paragliding schools.
https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mi ... 000001&z=4

MAPS (Not Downloadable):

Paragliding Earth:
Very detailed map, focused mostly on paragliding, but does contain hang gliding information too. Focused on flying sites, but does not distinguish schools or clubs.
https://www.paraglidingearth.com

Paragliding Map:
Very detailed map, focused mostly on paragliding, but does contain hang gliding information too. Focused on flying sites, but does not distinguish schools or clubs.
https://www.paraglidingmap.com

You can see many launches with windy.com
It uses data from xcontes.org flights I think.

The place I live offers a lot of what you want. Florianópolis-SC Brazil.
A bunch of smaller islands to dive, also near Bombinhas-SC known for diving spots and turtles.
Several surfing spots, kite/wind/foil surfing, anything water.
There's a whole mountain range starting about here and going south into the RS state: Serra do Tabuleiro, Serra Corvo Branco, Serra do Rio do Rastro, Serra da Rocinha (includes a launch), then it's RS. The whole state is nice really.

Regarding HG, in Florianópolis there's a beautiful site called Lagoa da Conceição, that's my local, some 50-60 flying days per year. Beach flights at Brava, and some other less frequent sites. In the continent theres Canelinha (Morro da Pipa) with some XC possibilities and Santo Amaro (Morro do Queimado) which offers flights over Pico do Tabuleiro and Cambirela.

If you are planning to retire, your money is worth 5x here in Brazil. You can really live a nice life with $1k per month, including rent, food and gas for flying. You can always travel around and fly in places like Brasilia, Pico do Gavião (Andradas-MG), Carmo do Rio Claro, Rio, and so many others including Tacima and Quixadá in the northeast. The world record for foot launched free flight is 690km by Glauco Pinto from Tacima.

You can always drive down to Argentina where everything is a lot cheaper at the moment, very cheap even for brasilians. There are places to fly over there too.
