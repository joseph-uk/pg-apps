What Is Video Analytics?

The brains of your security system

Video Analytics, also known as Video Content Analysis (VCA), is a technology that utilizes algorithms to automatically analyze video content.

Video Analytics solutions perform real-time video processing to automatically generate descriptions (metadata) of what is happening in a video stream. This allows them to perform tasks such as detecting intruders, reading license plates, counting people and tracking objects.

Video Analytics in Security

In the physical security industry, Video Analytics software is a pivotal tool, revolutionizing surveillance and threat detection. By automating the tedious task of analyzing extensive CCTV footage, it greatly enhances the efficiency of security personnel. The generated metadata forms the basis for informed decision-making, empowering security staff to respond immediately to potential threats.

Seamless integration with a video management system (VMS) facilitates surveillance automation by allowing the creation of rules for specific events, alerting security personnel for immediate action.

Types and Applications of Video Analytics

Video Analytics can be applied in various ways, including object detection and object tracking, motion detection, crowd detection, people counting, loitering detection, detection of suspicious behavior, intrusion detection for theft prevention, identification of individuals driving in the wrong direction, vehicle counting, license plate recognition, fire and smoke detection, and much more.

AI-Based Video Analytics

We leverage the power of artificial intelligence (AI) to enable users to understand their environment more deeply so they can respond proactively. And ultimately, predict unforeseen or future situations. Developed on Bosch's intellectual property used in automotive applications, like autonomous driving, we have over 20 years of experience developing algorithms for Video Analytics.

Faster Than Humans Can Detect

Faster Than Humans Can Detect

An AI-enabled Video Analytics camera identifies potential security incidents faster than security staff can. It monitors and detects events in real time, which is essential for critical incidents such as break-ins. In addition, the video camera only alerts when necessary, allowing operators to focus on other important tasks. Some examples of use-case specific solutions include early detection of traffic jams, facilitating rapid responses after gunfire is detected, and uninterrupted tracking of moving objects in crowded scenes.

Self-Learning for Higher Precision

Self-Learning for Higher Precision

The latest advances in artificial intelligence (AI) use machine learning and deep learning. More powerful hardware and ever-increasing volumes of video data are the main drivers for deep learning models. They give video surveillance cameras the ability to self-learn and enable embedded Video Analytics to be taught to detect customer-specific objects or situations and to better understand their surroundings. Deep learning-based Video Analytics enhances detection capabilities in congested scenes and ignores potential disturbances such as vehicle headlights or shadows, extreme weather, and sun reflections. It drives higher precision detection capability and delivers accuracy levels beyond 95 percent.

Systematic Review Automation

Systematic Review Automation

Machine learning enables devices like video surveillance systems to better understand their surroundings and automatically review rules and certain tasks through Intelligent Video Analysis. They can transform a task from requiring human input to successful automation, resulting in faster and more accurate performance. It also allows devices to tackle more complex tasks with greater efficiency.
