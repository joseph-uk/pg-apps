Hey there george, I am one of the original pilots of Vallarta and yelapa area, been there many times over 20 years and love it to bits. Flying the Himalayas this year however! Conchas chinas can be fantastic everyday in the winter months,but tricky to find the ramp launch, either taxi or walk up to the highest point ( huge new white hotel tower)??? Then walk past hotel where development ceased, around the corner when you find 150 concrete stairs going straight up to a water tank, do it, you're on track, follow your nose up thru jungle, it's kinda obvious. In 10-14 minutes you will find the steel and mesh ramp ( ingenious, no rotor). Straight out to sea is a big public beach that your boring non flying friends can wait for you at...you can also hike all along that shore line up over the rocks to old town Vallarta beach or if you get really high, surf the city and find a non crowded beach to park it! For landing setup, your sea breeze on a nice day which is almost continuous thru winter will be blowing from yelapa or sw, it's like guaranteed!!! I know I use to tow tandem off the beach in Vallarta.
If you aren't adventurous, look up dan at macmexphotography.com, great guy and say hi from miguel.
Yelapa, long story but awesome culture, not big but reliable flying. 10am ish, you go up to tapa 2200' and usually launch by noon, afternoons you hike up to tapita 500' and often soar for hour or two? I have soared up to very top of mountains from lower many times but DO NOT GO DOWN IN THAT JUNGLE, if you do, you might want to slice your wrists before the snakes and creatures get you!!!!!
Have fun. Ask around for Pamela the vet at shambala or allen and les ( who boat tow)
Cheers

Posted: Thu Jan 30, 2014 1:38 UTC    Post subject: Follow report
Site brief. Getting there: Finding the base or starting point is easy by asking and Taxi driver to take you to the Hotel Grand Miramar and they will drop you at the main entrance to the hotel. From there walk down hill and take the first right up a dead end street. Take the street to the concrete steps and here is where the climb begins up to lunch.
At the top of the steps walk around behind the water tank up over a downed tree and stay on the trail. The trail was opened recently when I was there and be sure to look for the sharp turns. It is easy to follow the other foot paths off to no one knows. You should find an machete marks on trees after a bit. This will lead you up to launch.

The launch is great for were it is. Its small and little room for a forward launch, not that I would recommend it. During my visit late January it was soar-able from noon til dusk. The cycles start slow but onec they get going hold on spend some time observing the cycles and try to avoid launching at the peek of a cycle verses the end. The cycles will be quite lively and will lift you straight off launch. Look to the coastal birds to get an idea of just how active it is. When you see them cruising at eye level or higher you are in the right spot at the right time.

Lift was abundant with clouds forming at around noonish or later, good stuff! Cloud suck is a real hazard and don't get sucked in flying out over the ocean a little will help get you out of the strong lift and keep you from finding rocks in the clouds. Keep in mind too PV International is not far North from this location, and IR traffic for small aircraft commonly approach from the south west up the coast. Hope this helps someone someday, it seem like a great site but a little advanced for those with vary low hours. Get a season or two under your wing before seeking this kind of adventure!!

Made it up to Conchas Chinas and had a short flight! Cool launch, very unique site. Props to whoever built it. Definitely not for beginners, it is small and committing. I spent a couple hours ripping weeds to make enough space to lay out my wing. It definitely could use some more work, I wasn't able to lay out as far up the hill as I would have liked due to bushes growing through the mesh. The palm tree in front of the launch is tall, seems like sawing it down would be ideal.
Launch was pretty easy to find following the directions posted above. Trail was in good shape and led right there. Coordinates of the launch as found on paragliding map are 20.5872,-105.2380.
Conditions ended up being light so it wasn't a soaring flight but I had a nice landing at the Hyatt to a round of applause from everyone in the swimming pool and a friendly escort by security out of the hotel grounds. All in all a fun adventure, and worth tagging onto a trip to Yelapa.
