Posted: Mon Nov 25, 2024 14:05 UTC    Post subject: RE: SeeYou Navigator for iOS and Android
I have used Flyskyhy on Iphone for several years, but have now switched to Navigator. During a 3 week paragliding holiday in Bir Billing in October, I met a group of 4 pilots who were already using Navigator, and they convinced me. I did an evaluation via the free download, and then took a subscription. Because of the clear advantages compared to Flyskyhy :
* better maps (Terrain display in Himalayas was very good)
* semi transparent navboxes that still allow a big view on the map
* in flight easy selection of targets to navigate to (mountain tops, take-offs, landings, villages, waypoints from collections, ..., flight mates on OGN, but also a random point on the map)
* Airspace information easily accessible on the map
* The kk7 thermal hotspots can easily be placed on the map (not available in FSH)

What am I missing (or have not discovered yet) :
* I miss the option to add or save user target waypoints to a waypoint collection, so that they can easily be retrieved later to include in a route or task.
(this can be done more or less in a more complicated way via SeeYou Cloud, but I prefer to be able to do this directly via the iPhone)
* Waypoints from Waypoint cup collections usually display a code (for example B01, B02, ...). Being able to change the name or clarifying description directly on the iPhone would be nice. In the case of Bir Billing for example "Trainstation", "Golfcourse", "Takeoff Billing", etc ...
* GPS fix via iPhone sensors, without a connected XC Tracer, mostly does not work. This has already been a previous comment on this forum. It does (sometimes) work by turning the Location service for Navigator off and on again in the iPhone settings. Google maps and Flyskyhy always have an quick iPhone GPS fix, so why not Navigator ...?

It's a nice piece of software that I hope it will be further improved in the future based on user feedback, as has happened in the past.
