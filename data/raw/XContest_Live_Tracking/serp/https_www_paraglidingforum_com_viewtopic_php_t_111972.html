I just came across this in the Cross brands livetracking in flight computers thread and think it deserves its own thread:

puretrack wrote:
Hi a friend pointed me at this discussion, and thought you might like to check out the system I'm developing at the moment. It's designed to solve a lot of the issues that have been raised.

https://puretrack.io/

It's a tracking system for aircraft including paragliders, but is designed to tie together all the different tracking systems into one place. At present we support:
- FLARM / OGN (including Fanet through the OGN).
- ADSB including ADSBhub and FR24 in select locations.
- Cellular apps including Overland, SeeYou Navigator, and any other OGN apps.
- The PureTrack iPhone app, which is handy if you switch aircraft/names often (iphone only at this stage).
- SPOT and InReach.

How you can use it:
- In flight, to see all aircraft around you including powered aircraft and gliders.
- On the ground, so ground crews can follow you. Or just watch your mates flying. And see who's launched on a day.
- PureTrack isn't limited to flying aircraft, your ground crew can show up too, so pilots can see where they are.
- If someone goes missing, you've got the latest data from any possible source in one place.
- We have a dedicated freeflight button up top to filter out non paraglider/hanglider aircraft quickly. So it's easy to see who's launched on a day in your area.

I'd be very interested in adding more features specifically for paragliding as there is demand. Some features coming soon:
- Live wind on the map from various sources.
- Ability to easily re-label FLARM/OGN names for paraglider pilots and other non-aircraft.
- Import data from LiveTrack 24 and other sources, let me know what's useful!
I come from a gliding world rather than paragliding, so I'm after improvements and ideas that would make it the ultimate paragliding tool too.

puretrack wrote:
I'd be very interested in adding more features specifically for paragliding as there is demand. Some features coming soon:
- Live wind on the map from various sources.

I'm not sure another site offering this is worth the effort. Personally, I rather look at Windy and also see the forecast, rain radar, satellite images, webcams etc. that provide a more complete weather picture. Or Burnair (Europe/Alps only)
puretrack wrote:
- Ability to easily re-label FLARM/OGN names for paraglider pilots and other non-aircraft.

Nice one!
puretrack wrote:
- Import data from LiveTrack 24 and other sources, let me know what's useful!

- Livetrack24 is probably the lowest common denominator since it's around for so long and many apps can track directly to LT24. Definitely a good thing as it allows many pilots to start using puretrack.io without changing their flight deck in any way.
- XContest live tracking becomes more and more popular, probably soon the 2nd popular live tracking service in PG, if not already.
- Have a look at popular apps like FlySkyHy, XCTrack, XCSoar, TopHat etc. - try to support whatever they support.
puretrack wrote:
I'm after improvements and ideas that would make it the ultimate paragliding tool too.

To become "the ultimate paragliding tool" is a high goal, you'd have to compete against Burnair (at least in the Alps) and they've set the standard really high already. I'd recommend to focus on becoming the ultimate live tracking tool instead...
- by integrating as many live tracking sources as possible (LT24 and XContest would make a great start!)
- by offering an easy to use API for other apps to directly track to puretrack.io (I rather not run another tracking app in parallel to my chosen flight computer.)
- by offering the best end user experience (on first glace, puretrack.io is already much better than LT24, OGN and XContest, imho, Keep that up!).
